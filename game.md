🎭 Sell Me This Pen (Funny Version) – How to Run the Game

This is a quick, funny role-play game where participants pretend to be salespeople and try to sell a random object in a creative or ridiculous way.

🎯 Objective

Participants must convince the audience to “buy” a random item by giving a funny sales pitch in 30 seconds.

The goal is not realism, but creativity and humor.

🪜 Step-by-Step Instructions
1️⃣ Ask everyone to pick a random object

Each participant quickly grabs any item near them.

Examples:

Pen

Coffee mug

Notebook

Headphones

Water bottle

Mouse

Sticky notes

2️⃣ Give them 30 seconds to think

Tell them:

“You are now a professional salesperson. You must convince us to buy this product.”

They have 30 seconds to prepare.

3️⃣ Each person gives a 30-second pitch

Participants present their product like a dramatic advertisement or TV commercial.

Encourage:

exaggeration

jokes

fake features

storytelling

🎤 Example (Pen)

Example pitch:

“Ladies and gentlemen, this is not just a pen.
This is the Productivity 3000.
When you hold this pen during meetings, everyone thinks you're taking important notes — even if you're drawing dinosaurs.”

😂 Another Example (Coffee Mug)

“This mug is not for coffee.
It's for emotional survival during Monday meetings.
The larger the mug, the smaller your problems appear.”

🤣 Example (Laptop Charger)

“This charger is the lifeline of remote workers.
Without it, your laptop dies… and suddenly you're free from meetings.”

🏆 How to Choose the Winner

At the end, vote for:

Funniest pitch

Most creative product

Best acting

People can vote by:

raising hands

chat reactions

poll

⏱ Recommended Time

5–10 participants → 10–15 minutes total

💡 Tips to Make It Funnier

Give a theme, like:

Sell the product like a luxury item

Sell it like a tech startup product

Sell it like a late-night TV ad

Example:

“Introducing the Smart Mug 2.0 — now with AI-powered coffee detection.”

✅ Why this game works well

Everyone can participate

No preparation required

Encourages humor and creativity

Works great in virtual meetings

🦸 Corporate Superhero Audition – How to Run the Game

This is a fun creativity game where colleagues imagine having a superpower that would help them survive office life. The twist is that the superpower should be funny, slightly useless, or exaggerated.

🎯 Objective

Participants must invent a funny office-related superpower and explain it in 20–30 seconds.

The goal is to make the group laugh or relate to the situation.

🪜 Step-by-Step Instructions
1️⃣ Introduce the game

Tell the group:

“Imagine you suddenly gained a superpower that helps you in office life. What would it be?”

Explain that it should be funny or weird, not a normal superhero power.

2️⃣ Give them 30 seconds to think

Participants quickly think of a creative superpower related to work situations like:

meetings

emails

deadlines

remote work

3️⃣ Each person presents their superpower

They explain:

What the power is

How it helps in office life

Example format:

“My superpower is ______. It helps me because ______.”

😂 Example Superpowers

1️⃣ Meeting Predictor

“My superpower is predicting when a meeting will be cancelled before I even join.”

2️⃣ Fake Listening Mode

“I can look like I'm listening in meetings while actually thinking about lunch.”

3️⃣ Email Teleportation

“I can instantly move emails from my inbox to someone else’s responsibility.”

4️⃣ Deadline Time Freeze

“Whenever someone says ‘urgent’, time freezes so I can finish the work.”

5️⃣ Meeting Escape Button

“I can disappear whenever someone says ‘Let’s go around the room and introduce ourselves.’”

🏆 How to Choose the Winner

After everyone shares, vote for:

Funniest superpower

Most relatable

Most creative

Voting methods:

chat reactions 👍

quick poll

raise hands

⏱ Recommended Time

10 participants → about 10 minutes

10. Meeting Bingo

Create a bingo board with phrases like:

“You’re on mute”

“Let’s circle back”

“Quick question”

“Can you see my screen?”

During the meeting people mark them. First to get Bingo wins.

7. Office Bingo

Create a bingo card with squares like:

“You’re on mute”

“Can you see my screen?”

“Let’s take this offline”

“Per my last email”

People mark squares when they hear them during the meeting. 😂

3. Common Things Challenge

Split people into small groups.

Give them a challenge:

“Find 5 things all team members have in common (NOT work related).”

Example answers:

Everyone hates Monday

Everyone likes pizza

Everyone has broken a phone

Groups present their answers.
